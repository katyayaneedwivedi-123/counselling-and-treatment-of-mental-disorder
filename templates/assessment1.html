Depression Assessment

Major depression

: 70%

This is the most common type of depression, and it is characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and worthlessness. People with major depression may also lose interest in activities they used to enjoy, have trouble sleeping or eating, and experience changes in their energy levels

Persistent depressive disorder

: 60%

This is a type of chronic depression that is less severe than major depression, but it lasts for at least two years. People with dysthymia may experience some of the same symptoms as people with major depression, but they are less intense.

Bipolar disorder

: 60%

This is a mental health condition that is characterized by alternating episodes of mania and depression. During manic episodes, people experience elevated energy levels, increased mood, and decreased need for sleep. During depressive episodes, they experience the same symptoms as people with major depression.

Postpartum depression

: 60%

This is a type of depression that can develop after giving birth. It is thought to be caused by a combination of factors, including hormonal changes, sleep deprivation, and the stress of new parenthood.

Seasonal affective disorder

: 60%

This is a type of depression that occurs during the winter months, when there is less sunlight. It is thought to be caused by a disruption in the body's circadian rhythm.

Psychotic depression

: 60%

This is a type of depression that is characterized by delusions and/or hallucinations. Delusions are false beliefs that a person holds even when presented with evidence to the contrary. Hallucinations are sensory experiences that occur in the absence of an external stimulus

Premenstrual dysphoric disorder

: 60%

This is a type of depression that occurs in the week or two before a woman's menstrual period. Symptoms of PMDD can include mood swings, irritability, anxiety, and depression.

Atypical depression

: 60%

This is a type of depression that is characterized by symptoms that are different from those of typical depression. For example, people with atypical depression may experience weight gain, increased appetite, and hypersensitivity to rejection.

Situational depression

: 60%

This is a type of depression that is caused by a specific life event, such as the death of a loved one, a job loss, or a divorce. Situational depression usually resolves on its own within a few months, but it may require treatment if it lasts longer than that.

Disruptive mood dysregulation

: 60%

This is a mental health condition that is characterized by chronic irritability and frequent anger outbursts in children and adolescents. DMDD can cause significant problems in a child's life, including academic difficulties, social problems, and aggressive behavior
